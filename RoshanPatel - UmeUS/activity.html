Activities

Kayaking

Kayaking is a fun activity that involves moving through water in a small water vessel with the aid of a double-bladed paddle. It allows the boat driver to maneuver through waterways by sitting face-forward and propelling ahead with alternating side-to-side paddle strokes. The paddler sits in the cockpit with the legs extended beneath a closed deck, leaving the upper body free and exposed. Kayaking is the perfect way for people of any age or ability to engage with water sport. Usually taking place in safe, protecteed areas of water, there are many locations in UmeUS where you can go for a relaxing paddle. No experience is needed to engage in kayaking, and it is a perfect activity for childen.

Wall Climbing

Wall climbing is an artificially constructed wall with grips for hands and feet, usually used fro indoor climbing. Some are brick or wooden constructions, but the material most often used on most modern wall is a thick multiplex board with holes drilled into it. We provide wall climbing experience with the safety guidelines that help children become more mentally and phycially fit. Indoor rock climbing is one of the most innovative physical activities today. It not only builds physical skills, but also helps to develop social-emotional and academic skills.

Zip Line

A zip-line, is a pulley suspended on a cable, usually made of stainless steel, mounted on a slope. It is designed to enable cargo or a person propelled by gravity to travel from the top to the bottom of the inclined cable by holding on to, or being attached to, the freely moving pulley. It has been described as essentially a Tyrolean traverse that engages gravity to assist its speed of movement. Its use is not confined to adventure sport, recreation, or tourism, although modern-day usage tends to favor those meanings. Zipline for kids is an excellent sports activity in which the kids goes from one side of the wire firmly fixed on the poles or trees to the other using trolleys mounted on the wire. The name, Zipline, came from the 'zip' sounds as the kids travel from one side to the other.
